How will I learn to code if I could start over again

1 October 2022
Written on my personal blog.

    I'm sure you've come across the same title in many videos/blogs. But every person has a different story and different learnings. And this is mine. I hope you can take away at least a couple of learnings from this blog. Let's start with the 5 mistakes I did and how you could avoid them.

  1. Not sticking to a single resource :

        The Internet is abundant with resources to learn pretty much anything. And the coding community is very strong. Every day I'd pick a new creator. I'd jump from one resource to another without actually completing the previous one. This led to chaotic and messy learning. If I could start over, I'd pick only a single resource and stick to it until it is completed.

  2. Not having a personalized plan/roadmap :

        Sure, you have had many people guiding you about what to learn, how to learn, and where to start. Even, I trusted othersâ€™ roadmap blindly. But that got me nowhere. Since there are many roadmaps out there, I wasn't able to follow one of them completely. Part of it comes from the lack of trust or fear of missing out. But this time, I'll do my due diligence and come up with my plan (Of course, I'll lift from various resources - that's how the learning happens ðŸ™‚ )

  3. Not practicing enough :

        Every other creator advises practicing more. But, everyone feels this. We watch a tutorial or read a blog, it makes us feel like we know things, that we don't need to practice them. But trust me, it is because of the creators who have simplified things so much that we think it is easy for us, but in reality, the creators themselves went through the learning curve and made things easy for themselves. So, be it a small thing or a big thing, I'll practice it anyway. There's no harm in practicing you know :)

  4. Getting into tutorial hell :

        It's been almost a year since I first heard of these words, TUTORIAL HELL. Let me spell it out for you. The tutorial hell is real and it's tough to not only get out of it but to even realize that we are stuck in one. This can be solved easily by choosing the right plan to study. The best way is to choose project-based learning and put in equal time out of the course on projects that are your own. Google for projects. You'll find many project titles. Just take the idea, and make the code.

  5. Not documenting my journey :

        I wasn't sure about documenting my learning. Even before trying this for real, it felt as if I'm creating educational content and that I can't do it every day like others. But I was wrong. Documenting doesn't necessarily mean that you should get likes and followers for your content. It means letting the world know that you are learning irrespective of whether the world cares about you or not. As I'm reaching the end of this article, I realize documenting is fun. It need not be a chore to do as I thought. Keep aside your fears and start expressing yourself, this may or may not help other people. But this will help you in unexpected ways for sure. Go for it. Do not think that they can only be blogs or videos, you can make posts, tweets, or anything you want. Take the plunge and you'll find your way eventually.

That's it for today's blog. I hope you like this and you learned something from this.

Thank you.
