My journey of completing Neogcamp Level Zero

1 October 2022
Written on my personal blog.

    There are many things I learned from Neogcamp. First, I want to take a moment to appreciate myself for successfully completing level Zero projects. For those who don't know about Neogcamp, it is a full-stack development Bootcamp. To enter into it, you must complete level zero and submit your projects. Don't worry theyâ€™ll teach you how to make and what to make.

    I heard about this a year ago. But I didn't try it back then. And I still regret not giving it a shot. As a 2022 graduate without a job, I was searching frantically for a job and learning simultaneously to crack something big. Thatâ€™s when I came across the alumni of Neogcamp on Twitter. The community they built was very warm and comforting. Then I saw some more posts and soon I wanted to be a part of that. Unfortunately, it had only 2 cohorts every year. Fortunately, the admissions weren't open yet and the next cohort was in Jan 2023. Realizing it's not too late, I started this on the 5th of September 2022.

    Previously, I took a couple of courses on Web development and I knew I hold some interest in that domain. I even completed a few courses. I made a few projects which included the backend as well. I made a complete full-stack application by end of a course. But my learning was not up to the mark. I followed everything they said and knew what to do when a project is started. Yet, I was unable to make projects on my own.

    For the first lesson, I was very thrilled at the idea of making something in just 2 hrs and being able to show that app to my friends and family. And I did find it quite fun. By taking Tanay's (the instructor) tips, I coded after the tutorial and not while watching it. And I completed the projects without finding the need to watch them again. That was very refreshing and boosted my confidence a lot. I felt as if I'd done something. The cherry on the cake is that all this is free of cost. Yes, you heard it right, it is available for free on youtube and the only things you need to put in are your time and effort.

    You can complete this in less than 20 days. I was a bit lazy and inconsistent due to my commitment to learning other things. It took me 20 days approx. At the end of 20 days, I have a ready-to-show portfolio with some small but cool projects which can demonstrate my skills better than a few words on my resume.

    I don't know if I'll get in for Level One, but I'm content with what I've done and learned from level Zero for now. Looking forward to leveling up and continuing my journey with neogcamp.
